There are a few resources that have really shaped how I approach teaching, specially in a classroom. Here's a list in no particular order.

# Salman Khan's Ted talks
The creator of Khan Academy has given [a couple ted talks](https://www.ted.com/speakers/salman_khan?language=en). They have very interesting ideas about teaching and learning in the 21st century.

# Ando Ko's talk on learning to code
Andy Ko is a professor at the University of Washington. His talk [Learning to Code](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mkzHIhKaUX4) is sort of a roundup on the current state of teaching in Computer Science. Hint: from what I understood, it's not good.

# Seth Godin's Stop Stealing Dreams
Reading Seth Godin's free book [Stop Stealing Dreams](https://medium.com/keep-learning-keep-growing/stop-stealing-dreams-4116c7dbff7b) openned my eyes to  how our current school system came to be.
